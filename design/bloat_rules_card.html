BLOAT
Rules reference
The basic idea
You are a circle. Your score is a tail.
Move around the arena and collect glowing pickups. Each pickup adds one segment to a chain trailing behind you — your score made physical. The longer your tail, the higher your score multiplier, and the harder you are to control.
Movement
The more you collect, the worse you turn.
Your movement speed never changes. But your turning speed degrades with every segment added. At zero segments you can spin on the spot. At 25+ segments, you are steering a freight train — you have to start turning long before you need to change direction. Plan your arcs early.
Enemies
They don't just want your head.
Chasers (red circles) — home toward your nearest segment, not your head. If one hits your body, it tears off everything behind the impact point — you lose that score, but survive. If one hits your head, you die instantly.

Cutters (acid diamonds) — appear after 90 seconds. They aim specifically for the middle of your chain to bisect it, denying you the multiplier. Appear rarely at first, then more often.
Scoring
Risk and reward are the same number.
Each pickup is worth base value × your current segment count. Zero segments = 1× value. 30 segments = 30× value. The multiplier resets whenever you shed or lose segments to an enemy. There is no cap.
Shed — [ space ]
You can sacrifice your score to survive.
Press Space to detach the back half of your tail. You instantly regain turning ability. But the dropped segments don't disappear — they freeze in place as solid obstacles for 3 seconds. You just rebuilt the maze around yourself. 4 second cooldown. Minimum 6 segments required.
Controls

Turn left / right
↑ ↓ / W S
No effect — speed is fixed
Space
Shed (sacrifice back half of tail)
R
Restart after death
Survive vs die
You survive if...
— enemies hit your body (not head)
— you shed before getting cornered
— you avoid your own ghost segments
You die if...
— any enemy touches your head
— you run into your own shed obstacle
— you get cornered with no room to turn
